Visual inspection is a non-destructive technique to detect flaws on
casting products. It involves an inspector looking at each test piece
with the naked eye and then classifying the product as either
defective or OK based on his assessment. Due to its reliance on
human factors, however, visual inspection is prone to
misclassification and can be time-consuming.

      This project explores automation of visual inspection with
computer vision. A deep learning model called convolutional neural
networks (CNN) is created to distinguish images of defective and
non-defective castings.
